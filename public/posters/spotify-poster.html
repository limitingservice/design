Spotify Un(Wrapped)

A UX Case Study on Music Discovery and Social Engagement

Fernando Luna

Human-Computer Interaction

Project Context

6 Weeks
5 Members
UX Research & UI Design

The Goal: Evaluate Spotify's usability, focusing on music-sharing communities and the impact of design choices on social engagement.

Demographics: College students with 3 to 11 years of Spotify Premium experience, selected to gauge both novice and expert behaviors.

Core Problems Identified

  • No native sharing: Users are forced onto third-party apps to share songs internally.
  • Mobile friction: Poor playlist management workflows on mobile devices, lacking vital song previews.
  • Navigation & Algorithms: Confusing navigation for downloading, and slow algorithmic learning curves resulting in repetitive recommendations.

Design Recommendations

  • Simplify Playlists: Enable drag-and-drop song rearrangement and provide song previews to expedite sorting.
  • Elevate Social Access: Introduce a dedicated "Friends" tab to surface social interactions and activity tracking out of hidden menus.
  • Quick-Add Gestures: Implement a long-press hold on the plus button to display an instant playlist assignment pop-up.

Future Opportunities

  • In-App Social Feed: A dedicated space where users can broadcast their current rotation to friends natively.
  • Section Commenting: Similar to SoundCloud, allow users to leave contextual thoughts on specific timestamps of a track.
  • Interactive Collaboration: Real-time playlist updates and in-playlist 'likes' to make shared music curation highly dynamic.
